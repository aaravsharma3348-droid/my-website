Frequently Asked Questions

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a method of investing in mutual funds where you invest a fixed amount regularly (monthly, quarterly, etc.). It helps in rupee cost averaging and disciplined investing, making it easier to build wealth over time without timing the market.

The ideal SIP amount depends on your income, expenses, and financial goals. A good rule of thumb is to invest 10-20% of your monthly income. Start with a comfortable amount like ‚Çπ1,000-‚Çπ5,000 and gradually increase as your income grows.

Main types include: Equity Funds (invest in stocks, higher risk-return), Debt Funds (invest in bonds, lower risk), Hybrid Funds (mix of equity and debt), and ELSS Funds (tax-saving equity funds with 3-year lock-in).

Mutual funds are regulated by SEBI and are relatively safe investment vehicles. However, they carry market risks. Diversification across different funds and long-term investing can help mitigate risks. Always invest based on your risk tolerance and financial goals.
